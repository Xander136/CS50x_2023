Hiragana

The word hiragana literally means "flowing" or "simple" kana ("simple" originally as contrasted with kanji).

Katakana

The word katakana means "fragmentary kana", as the katakana characters are derived from components or fragments of more complex kanji.

Learn more
Kanji

The term kanji in Japanese literally means "Han characters". The characters have Japanese pronunciations; most have two, with one based on the Chinese sound. A few characters were invented in Japan by constructing character components derived from other Chinese characters.
